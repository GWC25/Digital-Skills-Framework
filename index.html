General Digital Skills

Drawn from the DfE Essential Digital Skills Framework and the FutureDotNow AI-updated EDS (October 2025). The five core EDS categories — Communicating, Handling Information & Content, Transacting, Problem Solving, and Being Safe & Legal Online — cut across all three contexts and underpin every curriculum area at Weston College.

EIF relevance: The Inspection Toolkit explicitly requires evidence that learners develop "the digital skills needed to study curriculum and learning goals." These 15 skills represent the universal floor — every learner should reach these regardless of programme. The FutureDotNow October 2025 report confirms AI literacy must now be embedded across all five EDS categories.

Framing Digital Skills for Industry Needs

How digital skills are framed determines whether staff and learners engage or disengage. The most effective framing connects directly to an industry outcome learners already care about.

Golden Rule: Never teach the technology. Teach the task that technology enables.
